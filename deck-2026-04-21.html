Why this exists

Brenda's morning takes 30 minutes. Every morning.

She's a gastric cancer patient with a regimen that punishes a single forgotten pill. Standard reminder apps fire once and stop. So she built a paper system. Then a whiteboard. Then a phone tree of family members.

"I needed something that wouldn't give up on me."

Navilum is the system Brenda needed: a reminder that escalates, a caregiver loop that activates, and a schedule that understands oncology cycles — not just "every 8 hours."

Existing apps fire one notification. If you're in the shower, asleep, or in chemo brain — that dose is gone, and nobody knows.

The solution

A companion that escalates — to you, then to the people who love you.

Escalating notifications. Refires every N minutes until acknowledged. Provisional patent.
Chemo cycle scheduling. Native "21 days on / 7 off" and complex oncology regimens. Provisional patent.
OCR pill scanning. Point camera at a bottle or sheet — schedule auto-builds (Azure Computer Vision).
Caregiver swarm alerts. Missed dose fans out to designated contacts via push + SMS.
Voice-cloned alarm. Wake up to your spouse's voice saying your name, not a generic chime.
Visual dose verification. Snap the pill cup; Gemini vision confirms it matches the schedule.
Side-effect memory. Logs symptoms over time and surfaces medication correlations.
Offline-first. Reminders work with no signal. Sync when reconnected.
Regulatory posture

Designed to stay outside HIPAA scope at MVP.

Navilum is a scheduler and reminder companion. Users enter their own medication names and times. We do not store, transmit, or process Protected Health Information. No EHR integration, no lab data, no clinical records.

Why this matters to investors:

Ship in weeks, not quarters. No BAA gating. Clear path to HIPAA-grade architecture in v2 once we add the provider dashboard and clinic integrations — controlled, not retroactive.
