The 3 Roles AI Cannot Replace in Healthtech

🏥
Clinical / Regulatory Advisor
Medical accuracy, HIPAA decision-making, FDA regulatory strategy, and clinical credibility cannot be delegated to AI. One wrong call costs you everything.
🤝
Hospital Sales / BD
Enterprise healthcare sales is relationship-driven. Hospitals buy from people they trust. AI can prospect and research — but a human has to sit across the table and close.
⚖️
Legal / IP Attorney
Regulatory strategy, patent prosecution, BAA negotiations, and HIPAA compliance require licensed counsel. AI can draft — a lawyer must sign off.

Partnership Development

Four partnership verticals with approach strategy, proof requirements, timelines, and revenue mechanics.

🏥
Hospitals (B2B — The Revenue Engine)
  • Price: $150–200/patient/month. They bill insurance $140–151 (RPM/CCM). Net cost: ~$0–60.
  • Value stack: No-show reduction ($50–80), zero-error scheduling ($20–40), verified med adherence logs ($30–50), AI call automation ($20–40)
  • Approach: Innovation labs, MATTER Chicago connections, personal Advocate relationship
  • Targets: Advocate Lutheran General (personal), Northwestern (MATTER), Rush, UChicago Medicine
  • Timeline: 3–6 months for LOI, 6–12 months to pilot revenue
Revenue model
$150–200/patient/mo (hospital pays us, bills insurance $140+ back)
🛡️
Insurance / Payers
  • Approach: Innovation teams — Optum, Humana Studio H, Aetna
  • Show: Cost reduction, member engagement, compliance
  • Frame: "We reduce [cost metric] by X%"
  • Timeline: 12–24 months to contract
Revenue model
PMPM fees + outcome bonuses
💊
PBM Companies
  • Approach: Mid-tier first — Capital Rx, RxBenefits, Navitus
  • Show: Integration architecture, adherence data, cost reduction
  • Timeline: 9–18 mo (mid-tier), 18–36 mo (Big 3)
  • Big 3: CVS Caremark, Express Scripts, OptumRx
Revenue model
Per-referral fees ($1–5/filled Rx)
🔬
Pharma Companies
  • Approach: Oncology portfolios — Roche, Pfizer, Merck, AZ, BMS
  • Show: 5K+ active users, demographics, FDA/FTC compliance
  • Timeline: 6–12 mo (sponsored content), 12–24 mo (full programs)
  • Scale: CPM $15–50, sponsored $5–25K, programs $50–500K
Revenue model
CPM + content programs + data licensing

The Solopreneur-to-Scale Story

The narrative arc that turns authentic lived experience into a market-defining company.

This is the story of how one person, armed with AI tools and authentic lived experience, can build a company that transforms how 53 million American caregivers navigate the healthcare system. The opportunity is real. The timing is right. The tools exist. The only variable is execution.
1
The Personal Pain → The Insight
The founder lived the problem that 53 million caregivers face every day. That lived experience is an unfair advantage no competitor can fake — it drives authentic product decisions and opens doors that cold outreach never could.
2
Building with AI → Working Prototype in Weeks
Claude Code, n8n automation, and HIPAA-compliant APIs compress the build cycle from 6 months to 4–6 weeks. One founder with the right tools moves faster than a funded team with the wrong culture.
3
Validation Through Community → Proof
Caregivers test the product, hospitals sign LOIs, and organic word-of-mouth replaces a paid acquisition budget. Social proof from the community you're serving is the most credible signal you can show investors.
4
Funding on Authenticity + Traction → Resources
Raise from a position of strength — traction, community, and an authentic founder story beat a polished deck with no proof. The narrative arc from caregiver to CEO is a fundable story.
5
Scaling with People Where AI Can't Replace → Growth
Hire strategically into the three human-critical roles first. Let AI handle everything else until revenue justifies headcount. Every hire should increase revenue per employee, not decrease it.
6
Seven Revenue Layers Compound → Blue Ocean Dominance
Subscriptions, hospital licensing, RPM revenue, pharma partnerships, PBM referrals, marketplace GMV, and data licensing layer together into a defensible, diversified revenue engine no single competitor can replicate.
7
BELC Foundation → Mission-Driven Loyalty
The BELC Foundation provides free, full access to gastric and ovarian cancer patients — the most underfunded cancer types with zero dedicated support apps — no forms, no barriers, no fees. This program is a permanent commitment embedded in Navilum's operating model, generating authentic word-of-mouth in the highest-empathy segment of the market and reinforcing the founder's story at every investor touchpoint.

Critical Metrics Dashboard

The KPIs that define success at every phase. Track these obsessively — they are your early warning system and your fundraising story.

Phase 0–1 · Month 0–4 · Foundation
Waitlist Signups
Total validated email signups. Target: 500+ before launch. Proof of demand before you write a line of code.
Caregiver Interviews
Discovery interviews completed. Target: 50+ deep interviews to validate pain points and willingness to pay.
Demo Completions
Product walkthroughs completed. Target: 30+ demo sessions with potential customers and hospital contacts.
Hospital LOIs
Letters of intent from healthcare institutions. Even 1–2 LOIs dramatically de-risks the fundraise.
Phase 2 · Month 4–7 · Validation
Paid Conversion Rate
% of free users converting to paid. Target: 2–5% for B2C, 15–25% for B2B trial. Below 2% means pricing or value prop is broken.
Net Promoter Score
User satisfaction index. Target: 40+ (good), 60+ (excellent). In healthtech, NPS above 50 is a strong signal of product-market fit.
Subscription MRR
Monthly recurring revenue from subscriptions. Target: $5K MRR by end of Phase 2. $5K MRR = $60K ARR run rate.
Hospital LOIs (Signed)
Signed letters of intent with healthcare institutions. Target: 3–5 signed LOIs for Series A narrative.
Phase 3 · Month 7–12 · Build
AI Call Success Rate
% of AI-assisted calls completing without escalation. Target: 85%+. Key RPM product metric proving clinical value.
Adherence Improvement %
Medication / care plan adherence lift vs. baseline. Target: 15%+ improvement. This is your clinical outcome claim for pharma partnerships.
RPM Revenue
Remote Patient Monitoring monthly billing. Target: $10K+ MRR from RPM by Month 12. $140–210 per patient per month.
Monthly Churn Rate
% of paying users canceling per month. Target: under 3% monthly (under 30% annually). Above 5% monthly = retention crisis.
Phase 4 · Month 12–18 · Scale
Marketplace GMV
Gross merchandise value through the platform marketplace. Target: $50K GMV/month by Month 18. Take rate of 15–25%.
ARPU
Average revenue per user monthly. Target: $25–75 B2C, $200–500 B2B. Rising ARPU = expanding product value and upsell success.
CAC Payback Period
Months to recover customer acquisition cost. Target: under 12 months for B2C, under 18 months for B2B/enterprise.
LTV:CAC Ratio
Lifetime value vs. acquisition cost. Target: 3:1 minimum, 5:1+ for healthy SaaS. Below 2:1 means your unit economics are broken.
Phase 5 · Month 18–24 · Growth
Enterprise Contracts
Signed multi-year enterprise agreements. Target: 5–10 enterprise contracts by Month 24. Each contract worth $100K–$1M+ ARR.
Hardware Adoption Rate
% of users with connected device (wearable / RPM hardware). Target: 20%+ of active users. Hardware creates high-switching-cost moats.
Revenue per Employee
Total ARR divided by headcount. Target: $400K+ per employee. AI-native companies should maintain 2–5× the industry average.
Burn Multiple
Net burn divided by net new ARR. Target: under 1.5× (excellent under 1×). Burn multiple is the most important capital efficiency metric for Series B+.
